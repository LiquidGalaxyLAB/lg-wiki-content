(Text in bold from the slide, a normal one are notes taken from what he said)

→ **Showcase your LG rig and the p.o.c of the idea in the proposal docs** (Proof your understanding for the lg rig)  
→ **Mention the [GSOC Timeline](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1utIqs2n-XvGqDoX9VjgoHb3eV9TECvcTr0mj1NKlsic/edit#heading=h.oqgsontxm6yn) and how you will follow it** (write what you are going to do in the given period, tell how much work you will be done with in the first, second period, how much testing and finding bugs you will spend)

(Familiarize yourself with the codebase, how to setup a connection with lg, …)  
→ **Lastly, stay in touch with your mentors and admins for guidance**  
(The most important, communication is the key)

**The art of writing a good proposal.**  
  
It is the key to be selected for GSOC, it highlights how much you know about the organization  
  
→ Always make a copy of LG master doc mentioned at the beginning  
→ Indexing - Add a table of contents  
→ Describe your background - And your Interests!  
→ Project Description - Your Roadmap (How much time do you think it will take u)  
→ Create good mockups (In the project idea list on the website you have a chance to see those project, start on figma)  
→ Add some Uses Cases (Add significant real life examples, that help real people)  
→ UML diagrams are welcomed (Explained later in the document)  
→ Techstack and linked technologies (Examples are added later in the document)  
→ And most importantly, the timeline

_**Unified Modeling Language**_  
  
## What is UML?
  
It is a general-purpose visual modeling language that is intended to provide a standard way to visualize the design of a system. (It’s a set of rules specifically for drawing diagrams)  
  
**Refer to this link for example:** [Examples of UML diagrams - use case, class, component, package, activity, sequence diagrams, etc. (uml-diagrams.org)](https://www.uml-diagrams.org/index-examples.html)  
  
**I found these cool online tool, maybe it’s helpful for you:** [Online UML Diagram Tool (visual-paradigm.com)](https://online.visual-paradigm.com/diagrams/features/uml-tool/)  
  
Yash in the video was explaining a use case on how the user can create a connection for LG rig: refer to minute 14:00 in the [video](https://www.youtube.com/live/v8maFrJbp1c?si=gTSlPZykwh2-q0EI&t=866)  
The good thing about UML it’s easy and no need to master the design and you will find many videos to help you with that
