## Overview
\
The first and most important step is to join the discord of the community where you will find other contributors and mentors and introduce yourself in the community-chat channel, writing your name, country, university and the tech stack that you are working with.\
\
Going back to the community website you will find all the relevant deadlines for GSOC submissions and the tasks that need to be completed to be considered for GSOC engagement in the community through helping fellow contributors and the general community is as important as finishing the tasks in time as liquid galaxy is looking for long term involvement in a healthy and helpful communities so we can all help each other and grow together.\
\
Do not Dm mentors and ask all your questions on the community questions and answers channel as there are a lot of members in the community and the mentors cannot answer each one individually and so that all the other community members can also make use of the answers.\
\
To start you will first need to create your own liquid galaxy through virtual machines or using physical devices if you are able to of course. The community provides a lot of material to start with, like the documentation for liquid galaxy available on the website or the tutorial videos made by community members on YouTube which you will also find on the website.\
\
After setting up your liquid galaxy you can try your hand in making an application utilizing it. Of course, this is to grow your own personal experience in using it and you can access the repos of any past contributor to get some inspiration and to understand more about KML files as it is a very critical aspect of building applications for liquid galaxy.
